# ChartQuest — Game Director Audit
### Commercial-launch review of the first 15 minutes
*Reviewer hat: Lead Game Director (Nintendo / Supercell / Riot / Blizzard / Valve lens). Brutally honest. Core assumptions on the table.*

---

## 0. The one thing to read if you read nothing else

ChartQuest is currently **two games running in parallel that keep interrupting each other**:

- **Loop A — the platformer:** Finn auto-runs and the player taps/swipes to jump, boost, and dive across candle tops. This is *fun, kinetic, yours.*
- **Loop B — the school:** quick-reads, flash quiz, the bet, the trade panel, lesson cards, the trade-review cards, the boss "exercise." Every time Loop B fires, **the platformer freezes and a popup teaches you something.**

The player is having fun in Loop A, then the game stops them to run a flashcard in Loop B, then hands them back to Loop A. Start, stop, start, stop. That seam is where retention dies — not because any single screen is bad (they're polished now), but because **the game keeps switching from "I'm playing" to "I'm being taught."**

> **The breakthrough you're looking for is the same move that gave you Trader View: stop bolting trading *onto* the platformer and make the platforming *be* the trading.** Reading a candle should be the jump, not a popup that pauses the jump. When "should I jump?" literally means "is this green/up or red/down?", the player learns trading **with their thumbs**, never realizing it was a lesson. That is the difference between "I'm taking a course" and "I accidentally learned trading."

Everything below supports that thesis.

---

## 1. Overall Score

**Opening experience: 6.2 / 10**

- Production value, charm, and the Finn/Trader-View identity are genuinely **8–9/10** — above most edu-games and many indie games.
- Pacing, agency timing, and the teach-by-popup model drag it down to a **5/10** on "is this fun second-to-second."
- It currently reads as *"a beautifully produced trading course wearing a platformer costume,"* when the goal is *"a platformer that happens to be trading."*

The ceiling here is a 9. The gap is structural, not cosmetic.

---

## 2. Five-Minute Retention Score

**~55–60% would still be playing at 5:00.**

**Why they stay:** Finn is adorable, the chart-as-world is novel, the engineered first win + Trader-View zoom is a real dopamine spike, and the orange boss portal creates a clear "something big is coming."

**Why they leave:** the first ~90 seconds are *watch + read* (cinematic, "ESCAPE THE MARKET" card, GREEN=UP/RED=DOWN, coach hints) before the player makes a decision that *matters*. Impatient mobile players (the majority) bounce in the first 30–60 seconds if they're reading instead of doing. The flash quiz and quick-reads, while clean now, feel like **school inside the game.**

---

## 3. Fifteen-Minute Retention Score

**~35–45% would still be playing at 15:00.**

**Why it drops:** by minute 8–12 the player has seen the core Loop B beats two or three times each (read → trade → read → review). The novelty is gone and the *pattern* is exposed: *play a little, get quizzed, play a little, get quizzed.* The first boss is a **quiz with a health bar**, not a gameplay climax, so the big anticipation the portal built **doesn't pay off in gameplay** — it pays off in more multiple-choice. That betrayal of expectation is the single biggest 15-minute churn driver.

This is the number that should scare you. 15-minute retention is your purchase-intent proxy, and a quiz-boss caps it.

---

## 4. Biggest Problems — Top 10 by impact

1. **Two interrupting loops (start-stop).** Learning is delivered by *pausing* the fun. #1 structural drag on retention. (Breakthrough target.)
2. **Real agency arrives too late.** Cinematic → premise card → GREEN/RED → coach → flash quiz → bet → *then* a real trade. The first ~3–4 minutes are mostly guided/auto. A new player should make a meaningful choice in the **first 15 seconds.**
3. **The boss is a quiz, not a climax.** The portal, music, and "GUARDIAN 1/10" promise an *encounter*; the payoff is a Trade-Prediction exercise. Anticipation → anticlimax.
4. **Reading load.** Lesson cards, the multi-step ENTRY→STOP→TARGET walkthrough, trade-review cards, prediction explanations. Individually fine; cumulatively it's a lot of *stop-and-read* for a mobile game.
5. **Repetition of the quiz beat.** quick-read → quick-read → quick-read reads like flashcards. The *verb* doesn't change, so it feels like drilling, not adventuring.
6. **No discovery or exploration.** It's a straight corridor. Nothing is hidden, optional, or surprising. Players never feel clever for finding something.
7. **The forced 2:1 wins risk feeling hollow.** Great for confidence, but if the player senses there's no real downside, the "win" dopamine decays fast. The rails must be invisible.
8. **Uneven dopamine cadence.** Tight during trades/celebrations; long flat stretches during lessons/reads. Gaps >15–20s between rewards in the teaching segments.
9. **The fantasy is *told*, not *felt*.** "Escape the Market Maker / Block Chain" is delivered in a cinematic and a card. During play, the antagonist is invisible — the world doesn't feel oppressed by anyone.
10. **Mobile modality tax.** Popups cover the chart you're supposed to be reading; lots of modal taps ("TAP TO CONTINUE", "GOT IT", "NEXT ROUND"). Each modal is a tiny tax on momentum.

---

## 5. Biggest Opportunities — Top 10 by expected retention gain

1. **Collapse reading into movement ("every jump is a read").** The breakthrough. Make candle-reading the platforming verb so learning is embodied. *Largest single retention lever.*
2. **A real decision in the first 15 seconds.** Before any text: "jump onto the green candle, dodge the red." Agency first, vocabulary second.
3. **Diegetic teaching — kill the modal lesson.** The candle *shows* the lesson (a green candle literally lifts Finn up; red drops him). One short in-world coach line, never a full-screen card.
4. **Make Boss 1 a gameplay gauntlet with rising tension** (the Gambler manipulates the candles in real time; you must out-*read* him while moving). Keep the quiz DNA but wrap it in play + spectacle.
5. **Add exploration & loot.** Shell caches on tall candles (rewards boosting), secret high jumps, optional "risk line" detours with bonus shells. Make boosting/tucking *worth it.*
6. **Tighten reward cadence to <10s.** Micro-rewards: shell sparkle on every few candles, combo chimes, "nice read!" micro-pops, near-miss slow-mo.
7. **A reading combo/streak meta.** Consecutive correct reads build a multiplier (shells/XP). Turns repetition into a *mastery chase* — the Supercell move.
8. **Stretch to 8–10 min before the boss by adding PLAY, not lessons.** Earn it with a collect/explore/combo stretch and a mini-skill gate, so the boss feels *deserved.*
9. **Anticipation build to the boss.** The portal awakens over distance; the chart corrupts (candles glitch purple); music layers in; a whale-shadow passes; screen shake on approach.
10. **Personify the antagonist during play.** The Market Maker occasionally "shoves" a candle (a sudden red spike) with a taunt — so the enemy is *felt* long before the boss screen.

## 7. Revised Opening Flow (same learning goals, more game)

**Principle: teach the *verb* in 15 seconds, the *vocabulary* through play, and let the boss be the *payoff* of a skill the player already feels good at.**

**0:00–0:20 — COLD OPEN, you're already playing.**
No cinematic first. Finn is running. One instruction, in-world: *"Land on GREEN to rise."* Green candles lift him toward a shell; a red candle is a dip to avoid. The player makes 3–4 *real* reads with their thumbs before a single sentence of theory. (The cinematic becomes an optional "lore" unlock or a 5-second sting, not a gate.)

**0:20–1:30 — NAME WHAT THEY JUST DID.**
*Now* a one-line, in-world label appears as they do it: green = buyers/up, red = sellers/down. No modal. They already *feel* it; you're just giving it a word. Shells rain for correct reads → combo meter starts.

**1:30–3:30 — PLAY DENSITY + DISCOVERY.**
Open the corridor slightly: shell caches on tall candles (boost to reach), a secret high jump, an optional risk-line detour. Reading direction is now *survival + greed* (read right → climb to loot; read wrong → small, friendly stumble). Combos build. This is where the player thinks *"this is a fun game,"* not *"this is lesson 2."*

**3:30–5:30 — THE FIRST REAL TRADE as a *power unlock*, not a tutorial.**
Frame it as "you've read 10 candles right — you've earned a TRADE." Trader View zoom = the reward cinematic. Keep ENTRY/STOP/TARGET but teach each **on the candle, one tooltip at a time, only as it becomes relevant** (no upfront 3-step wall). Price visibly marches to the TP (already shipped) → TARGET HIT.

**5:30–8:30 — RISING TENSION + MASTERY.**
2–3 more trades interleaved with collect/combo play. The **Market Maker starts interfering**: candles occasionally glitch purple, a taunt flashes, the music gains a layer, the screen shakes a touch. The portal grows on the horizon. Reading is now under light pressure = flow state.

**8:30–10:00 — BOSS as a *gauntlet*, earned.**
The Gambler corrupts the chart live; the player out-reads him *while moving*, dodging his manipulated candles, landing reads to damage him. Same educational content (read direction / strong close / momentum) but delivered as a **boss encounter**, not a worksheet. Victory = world un-corrupts, shells burst, Guardian 1 falls.

Net effect: **first 15 minutes feel like an adventure that taught you to read a chart,** boss arrives ~9 minutes in and feels *earned*, and you never sat through a "lesson."

## 8. Supporting Audits (condensed)

**Gameplay density (current vs. target):**
- *Reading text:* ~30% → target **<10%**
- *Watching (cinematic/celebrations/reveals):* ~20% → **~10%**
- *Moving/jumping/collecting:* ~25% → **~45%**
- *Trading/deciding:* ~15% → **~25%**
- *Waiting/modal taps:* ~10% → **<5%**
The headline: **double the "moving + deciding," halve the "reading + waiting."**

**Dopamine cadence:** trades/celebrations are great; the lesson and 5-read stretches go 15–25s between hits. Target **<10s** everywhere via micro-rewards (shell sparkles, combo chimes, near-miss slow-mo, "nice read!" pops). Add a visible **combo multiplier** as a constant low-grade reward.

**Exploration:** currently ~0. Add (a) shell caches that reward boosting onto tall candles, (b) 1–2 secret jumps per stretch, (c) optional risk-line detours (more shells, slightly riskier reads), (d) tiny environmental surprises (a whale shadow, a glitching candle). Players must occasionally feel *clever*, not just *correct*.

**Boss build-up:** today the portal is the only build-up and it's good but isolated. Layer it: portal awakens over distance → chart corruption creeps in → music adds tension → screen shake on approach → a Gambler taunt. Make the player *want* to dive in.

**Mobile UX:** biggest wins — (a) stop covering the chart with popups (teach in-world), (b) reduce modal taps (auto-advance where safe), (c) keep all controls in the bottom thumb zone (good already), (d) cap text per popup to one line. The dvh/safe-area and one-line-fit fixes shipped this week removed the worst offenders; the remaining tax is *frequency* of modals, not their layout.

**Player psychology arc (target):** Curious (0:00) → Capable (0:30) → Having fun (2:00) → Powerful (first trade) → Greedy (loot/combo) → Tense (Market Maker interferes) → Triumphant (boss). Today the arc sags into *Dutiful/Bored* during the read drills and *Confused/Letdown* at the quiz-boss. Close those two dips and the curve points up.

## 9. Launch Readiness — would I launch this?

**Not yet. I would hold launch.**

What's already **launch-grade**: Finn's identity, the chart-as-world concept, Trader View, the visual/audio polish, the live-BTC anchor, the engineered-first-win hook.

What **must change before launch** (in priority order):
1. **Collapse the two loops** — reading must become the moment-to-moment platforming verb, not a popup. (The single biggest change; everything else is smaller once this lands.)
2. **Real agency in the first 15 seconds** — playing before reading.
3. **Boss 1 becomes a gameplay encounter** with rising tension, not a quiz with a health bar.
4. **Halve the reading / modal load** — diegetic teaching, one-line coaches, fewer "TAP TO CONTINUE."
5. **Add discovery + a combo/streak meta** for density and replay pull.

Do those five and the opening goes from **6.2 → ~8.5**, and 15-minute retention from **~40% → ~65%+**. *Then* launch.

**The honest summary:** you have built a beautiful, polished trading *course* with a platformer's body. The remaining work isn't more polish — it's the one structural inversion that turns the course back into a game. You already proved you can find that kind of inversion (Trader View). This is the next one: **the chart is the level, and reading it is how you play.**
