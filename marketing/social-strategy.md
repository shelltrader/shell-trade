# Social Strategy — Shell Trade

> Platform-by-platform execution plan. Pairs with `marketing/content-pillars.md` (what we post) and `marketing/brand-voice.md` (how we sound). Each platform has its own agent (see `/agents/`) responsible for execution against this strategy.

## X (Twitter)

**Audience:** Crypto-native traders, indie builders, fintwit. Skews older and more sophisticated than TikTok; values specificity, numbers, and candor over polish.

**Content types:** Build-in-public updates, trading-education threads, market-education explainers, occasional memes, screenshots of boss fights and rank-ups with commentary.

**Posting frequency:** 1–2 original posts per day, plus active replies/quote-posts in the trading and indie-dev conversation throughout the day. Threads reserved for Trading Education and Market Education pillars (2–3 per week).

**KPIs:** Follower growth, link-clicks to the game, reply/quote engagement on threads (signal of credibility, not just reach), bookmark rate on educational threads (signal of save-for-later value).

**Growth strategy:** Lead with Build in Public and Trading Education — this is the audience that rewards transparency and competence over hype. Engage directly in trading/crypto-education conversations rather than only broadcasting. Quote-post relevant market moments with a Shell Trade angle rather than manufacturing unrelated hot takes.

## TikTok

**Audience:** Broad crypto-curious 18–30s, including many who have never traded. Highest top-of-funnel reach potential, shortest attention span, algorithm-driven discovery rather than follower-driven.

**Content types:** Trading Memes (highest-frequency format here), Turtle Journey teaser cuts, fast Trading Education hooks, Market Education in tight 30–45 second cuts.

**Posting frequency:** 1 post per day minimum, ideally 1–2. Consistency matters more than burst posting on this platform.

**KPIs:** Views, completion rate (watch-through), shares (the platform's strongest distribution signal), follower growth, profile-link clicks to the game.

**Growth strategy:** Memes and strong hooks first — earn the view, then let educational substance underneath build retention/follow intent. Ride relevant trends only when they fit the deadpan-turtle voice without breaking tone (see brand-voice do's/don'ts). Post consistently rather than perfecting fewer posts; the algorithm rewards volume and consistency on this platform more than any other.

## YouTube

**Audience:** Viewers actively seeking depth — either already trading and looking to fill knowledge gaps, or seriously considering starting. Search-driven discovery (SEO) matters as much as subscriber-driven discovery here.

**Content types:** Long-form Turtle Journey episodes, full Trading Education breakdowns, Build-in-public dev logs, and a Shorts feed carrying condensed cuts of TikTok-native content for cross-platform reach.

**Posting frequency:** 1 long-form video per week, plus 3–4 Shorts per week (repurposed from TikTok/Reels cuts).

**KPIs:** Watch time (the platform's core ranking signal), subscriber growth, search impressions/click-through rate on educational titles, Shorts-to-long-form conversion (viewers who watch a Short and then a long-form video).

**Growth strategy:** Treat titles and thumbnails as a discipline — this is the platform where SEO-style keyword thinking (e.g., "what is VWAP," "stop loss explained") drives discovery independent of any existing following. Build a back-catalog of evergreen educational explainers that keep earning search traffic long after publish day, distinct from the timely/episodic Turtle Journey content.

## Instagram

**Audience:** Visually-led, slightly more casual than X, skews toward Reels-driven discovery similar to TikTok but with a meaningfully large carousel/static-post audience for educational swipe-throughs.

**Content types:** Reels mirroring TikTok cuts, carousel breakdowns of Trading Education and Market Education threads, static Trading Memes, Stories for quick rank-up/boss-win moments.

**Posting frequency:** 1 feed post (Reel or carousel) per day, Stories used opportunistically for lower-effort moment-sharing (multiple per day when there's content).

**KPIs:** Reach, saves (strong signal for educational carousels specifically), Story completion rate, follower growth, link-clicks via bio/Stories link sticker.

**Growth strategy:** Repurpose aggressively from TikTok (Reels) and X (carousels from threads) rather than producing fully platform-native content from scratch — Instagram is a reach-multiplier on content already proven elsewhere, not a primary content-origination platform.

## Facebook

**Audience:** Older and more general-interest than the other four platforms; lower trading-sophistication on average, higher proportion of "parent/relative wants to understand what their kid is doing" — aligns closely with the "Worried Wendy" persona in `company-context.md`.

**Content types:** Market Education explainers (the most accessible pillar for this audience), Turtle Journey recap posts, occasional Trading Education content simplified further than the X/YouTube versions.

**Posting frequency:** 3–4 posts per week — this platform does not reward daily-posting volume the way TikTok/Instagram do, and over-posting here yields diminishing returns.

**KPIs:** Reach, shares (Facebook's strongest organic distribution signal, especially within groups), comments (community-style engagement skews older and more conversational here), click-throughs to the game.

**Growth strategy:** Lean on shareability within relevant Facebook Groups (parenting, personal finance, crypto-curious communities) rather than competing on raw algorithmic reach. Content here should assume zero prior context more than on any other platform — this audience is the least likely to already know what Shell Trade is.
